# Saracero lunfardo term

## Saracero

Lunfardo term (Argentine slang) describing a person who states something with **absolute conviction** despite it being untrue.

### Key distinction
- A **liar** knows they are lying.
- A **saracero** genuinely convinces themselves in the act — the conviction is real, which makes them more persuasive and dangerous than a liar.

### LLM parallel
Saraceros are structurally similar to LLMs: they assert things with high confidence that are false, not from deliberate deception but from a kind of auto-hypnotic conviction in the moment. The confidence is genuine even when the content is wrong.

### Philosophical connection
Related to Harry Frankfurt's *On Bullshit*: bullshit is indifference toward truth. The saracero goes further — not indifferent, but **actively self-convinced**. More dangerous than the liar precisely because conviction is real and transmissible.
